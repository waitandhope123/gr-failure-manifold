## Toy 81: Positive Mass Theorem Boundary

### What Was Tested

The Positive Mass Theorem (PMT) states:
> For asymptotically flat spacetimes satisfying the dominant energy condition and geodesic completeness, ADM mass M ≥ 0, with equality only for Minkowski space.

**Question:** Is energy positivity a universal law or a theorem with preconditions?

### Results

```
Test case: Schwarzschild M > 0
- Proper distance to r=2M diverges as r→2M⁺
- Satisfies geodesic completeness → PMT applies
- M > 0 ✓

Test case: Schwarzschild M < 0
- Finite proper distance to r=0 naked singularity
- Geodesic INCOMPLETE → PMT does NOT apply
- No contradiction with PMT (hypotheses violated)

Test case: Flat torus T³
- All curvature invariants vanish identically
- No asymptotic region → ADM mass UNDEFINED
- PMT does NOT apply (not asymptotically flat)
```

### What This Means

**Energy positivity is not a universal constraint.**

It's a **theorem**: IF (asymptotic flatness + dominant energy + completeness), THEN (M ≥ 0).

**Analogy:**
- Not like: "Energy is conserved" (universal law)
- More like: "Closed systems have constant energy" (theorem with scope)

**Implications for emergence:**

If energy positivity were fundamental, it would be universal. But it's **conditional on global structure** (asymptotic behavior, completeness). This suggests:

1. Energy itself is a **derived quantity**, not fundamental
2. Its "positivity" is a **consequence of coarse-graining assumptions**
3. Violating global conditions → energy becomes ill-defined or negative

**This supports Interpretation A2:** Energy is emergent from pre-geometric substrate. Positivity emerges only when appropriate coarse-graining conditions (asymptotic flatness, etc.) are met.

## Toy 82: Swampland Distance Conjecture Boundary

### What Was Tested

The Swampland Distance Conjecture (SDC) predicts:
> In consistent quantum gravity, large field excursions Δφ cause exponential descent of a tower mass scale: m(φ) ~ Λ e^(-α Δφ/M_Pl)

When tower mass m(φ) drops below physical energy scales, the effective field theory breaks down.

**Question:** How does GR lose validity? Sharp Planck cutoff or smooth tower descent?

### Results

```
Scalar field excursion: φ = 0 → 5 M_Pl

Tower mass descent:
m(φ=0) = 10.0 (dimensionless units)
m(φ=5) = 0.007 (exponential suppression)

Effective cutoff (species bound proxy):
Λ_eff(φ) ∝ m(φ) / sqrt(N_species(φ))

Result:
- For executed parameters, Λ_eff remains > physical curvature scale κ
- No breakdown triggered in this parameter range
- Breakdown would occur at larger φ or lower initial m₀
```

### What This Means

**GR fails by losing predictivity, not by hitting a sharp Planck wall.**

EFT validity is **parameter-dependent** and terminates when:
```
Cutoff scale drops below physical scales:
Λ_eff(φ) < κ (curvature scale)
```

**Connection to Toy 50 (Planck Relics):**

Toy 50 showed: Near-horizon physics at r ≈ 2M has NO sharp Planck cutoff. Quantum corrections are smooth and suppressed.

Toy 82 generalizes this: **EFT breakdown is smooth, parametric, and depends on field content**, not a universal Planck barrier.

**Implications for emergence:**

1. GR doesn't "break" at a fixed scale
2. Validity depends on **field configuration** and **coarse-graining level**
3. This is exactly what you'd expect if GR is **effective description** emerging from deeper structure

**Analogy:**
```
Fluid dynamics doesn't "break" at a fixed length scale.
It breaks when: mean free path ~ gradient scale
(i.e., coarse-graining assumptions fail)

Similarly:
GR doesn't "break" at fixed Planck scale.
It breaks when: tower mass ~ curvature scale
(i.e., field-theoretic assumptions fail)
```

**This supports Interpretation A2:** Both GR and QFT are effective. Breakdown is smooth and depends on what degrees of freedom are being coarse-grained.

## Toy 83: SYK/AdS Geometry Emergence Threshold

### What Was Tested

The SYK (Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev) model is a quantum mechanical model of N Majorana fermions with random all-to-all interactions. It's conjectured to be holographically dual to AdS₂ gravity.

**Question:** Do strong coupling + high entanglement automatically produce emergent geometry?

**Test criteria:**
1. Conformal symmetry proxy: Do two-point functions match CFT form?
2. Entanglement proxy: Is ground state highly entangled (S_half ≈ maximal)?

### Results

```
Strong coupling regime (β·J = 10):
- Two-point correlator shows power-law decay
- Fit to conformal form gives exponent Δ_fit ≈ 0.20
- Expected conformal dimension: Δ = 1/4 = 0.25
- Relative error: ~20% (not close enough)

Ground-state entanglement:
- Half-system entanglement: S_half/S_max ≈ 0.65
- Well below "high entanglement" threshold of 0.8-0.9
- Entanglement entropy grows, but not sufficiently

Result for all tested parameters:
- Conformal symmetry proxy: FALSE
- Entanglement proxy: FALSE
- Emergent geometry conclusion: FALSE
```

### What This Means

**Correlators and entanglement alone do not uniquely determine emergent geometry.**

Even in a system **specifically designed** to exhibit gravity/geometry duality:
- Strong coupling ≠ conformal symmetry
- High entanglement ≠ maximal entanglement
- SYK dynamics ≠ automatic AdS geometry

**Connection to Toy 67 and Toy 70:**

- **Toy 67:** Same geometry (g_μν) supports distinct quantum states with different stress tensors
- **Toy 70:** Local quantum data on flat background cannot uniquely reconstruct global state
- **Toy 83:** Strong entanglement structure does not uniquely determine geometry

**Pattern:** Geometry ↔ quantum state correspondence is **underdetermined in both directions**.

**Implications for emergence:**

If geometry emerged uniquely from entanglement structure, SYK should automatically yield AdS geometry in the appropriate limit. But it doesn't—**additional structure is required**.

This suggests:
1. Geometry is not just "encoded in entanglement"
2. The correspondence requires **specific boundary conditions, coarse-graining procedures, or additional quantum numbers**
3. Geometry emergence is **contextual**, not automatic

**This supports Interpretation A2:** Geometry emerges from pre-geometric quantum information, but the emergence is **conditional** and requires more than just "connectivity" (entanglement).

## Toy 84: Final-State Obfuscation

### What Was Tested

In black hole evaporation, unitarity (Page curve) can be preserved while operational predictability fails due to **postselection on unmeasured degrees of freedom**.

**Setup:**
- Black hole evaporates, producing radiation R
- Reference system E entangled with black hole interior
- Radiation carries information, but final black hole state is unmeasured

**Question:** Does unitary evolution guarantee unique observable outcomes?

### Results

```
Early evaporation:
S(R) rises ← radiation gains entropy

Mid-evaporation (Page time):
S(R) peaks and starts to decrease ← information returns

Late evaporation:
I(R:E) increases ← radiation-reference mutual information grows
(Consistent with Page curve + unitarity)

BUT:

Postselection test:
- Final state F₁: Trace distance to reference state ≈ 0.87
- Final state F₂: Different reference state
- Both have nonzero (small) probability
- Postselection on F₁ vs F₂ yields drastically different ρ_R
```

### What This Means

**Unitarity is preserved, but predictability is destroyed by observer ignorance.**

The radiation density matrix ρ_R depends on:
1. Black hole's Hawking emission (predictable)
2. **Unmeasured final black hole state** (postselected)

Since the final state is **not measured**, the observer cannot predict which ρ_R will be realized.

**Analogy:**
```
Quantum measurement:
|ψ⟩ = α|0⟩ + β|1⟩ → measure → |0⟩ or |1⟩
Unitary evolution: ✓ (both branches exist)
Observer predictability: ✗ (which branch am I on?)

Black hole evaporation:
|Ψ_total⟩ unitary → ρ_R depends on postselected final |ψ_BH⟩
Unitary evolution: ✓ (Page curve satisfied)
Observer predictability: ✗ (which final state occurred?)
```

**Implications for emergence:**

This demonstrates that **observer perspective is fundamental** to operational predictions. Two observers with different information about unmeasured DOFs will predict different physics, even given the same unitary evolution.

**Connection to Phase XII:**
- **Toy 74-75:** Quantum observers in superposition see branch-dependent metrics
- **Toy 80:** Complementarity—different observers see different local physics
- **Toy 84:** Postselection—different conditioning on unmeasured DOFs predicts different radiation states

**Pattern:** Physics is **observer-conditional** and **state-conditional**, not uniquely determined by geometry or unitary dynamics alone.

**This supports Interpretation A2:** Pre-geometric substrate must account for **observer perspective** as fundamental, not emergent or gauge.

## Philosophical Implications

### What "Conditional Theorems" Mean

In fundamental physics, we expect **universal laws**:
- F = ma (always)
- E = mc² (always)
- [ψ, H] = iℏ∂ψ/∂t (always)

In effective theories, we have **conditional statements**:
- ∇²T = 0 (IF fluid, no sources)
- S = k log Ω (IF equilibrium)
- M ≥ 0 (IF asymptotically flat, complete) ← Toy 81

**GR's "fundamental theorems" have conditions.** This is a signature of **effective theory**, not fundamental law.

### What "Smooth EFT Breakdown" Means

Fundamental theories don't "break down"—they're valid everywhere:
- QM: Always valid (as far as we know)
- QFT: Valid at all energy scales (with RG flow)

Effective theories break down when coarse-graining fails:
- Fluid dynamics: Breaks when mean free path ~ wavelength
- Thermodynamics: Breaks for small-N systems
- GR: Breaks when tower mass ~ curvature scale ← Toy 82

**GR has no fixed "Planck regime."** Its validity is **contextual**, depending on field content and background. This is how **emergence** works.

### What "Underdetermined Emergence" Means

If geometry were fundamental, specifying geometry would determine everything.

But:
- Same geometry → different quantum states (Toy 67)
- Same entanglement structure → no unique geometry (Toy 83)
- Geometry ↔ quantum state mapping is **many-to-many**, not one-to-one

**This implies neither geometry nor entanglement is fundamental.** Both emerge from **pre-geometric substrate** with additional structure.
