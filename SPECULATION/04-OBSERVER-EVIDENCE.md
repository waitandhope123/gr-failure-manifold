# Observer Evidence: Classical Observer-Dependence (Phases I-XI)

## The Central Pattern

**Many quantities in GR that seem "physical" are actually observer-dependent:**
- Horizons (appear/disappear with observer choice)
- Temperature (different observers measure different T)
- Time (proper time depends on worldline)
- Energy (depends on observer frame)
- Particle content (Unruh effect)

**This is radically different from pre-relativistic physics, and suggests these quantities are not fundamental.**

---

## Phase I-II: Basic Observer Dependence

### Toy 2: Gravity as Force vs Geometry

**Test:** Newtonian gravity vs curved spacetime description of same physics.

**Result:**
- Identical weak-field accelerations reproduced
- Redshift present in GR without Newtonian force analogue
- Observer-dependent attribution: "force" vs "geometry"

**What this means:**

Whether gravity is a "force" or "curved spacetime" depends on how you describe it.

**Implication:** The description is observer-dependent, not intrinsic to reality.

---

### Toy 4: Cosmological Redshift

**Test:** FLRW expansion, measure redshift.

**Result:**
- Redshift detected without peculiar motion
- Proper distances grow while comoving coordinates remain fixed
- No local force signal

**What this means:**

Redshift can occur without relative motion (Doppler).

**Observer choice:** Comoving observer sees no motion; distant observer sees recession.

**Implication:** "Motion" itself is observer-dependent in expanding spacetime.

---

### Toy 5: Rindler Horizons

**Test:** Uniformly accelerating observer in flat spacetime.

**Result:**
- Horizons appear in accelerated frame only
- Curvature invariants remain zero (spacetime is flat)
- Horizon location observer-dependent

**What this means:**

Horizons can exist without gravity (curvature).

**Key point:** Same spacetime (flat Minkowski), different observers (inertial vs accelerated), different physics (no horizon vs horizon).

**Implication:** Horizons are not intrinsic geometric features but **observer-dependent phenomena**.

---

### Toy 7: Schwarzschild Horizon Regularity

**Test:** Black hole horizon in Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates.

**Result:**
- Metric regular at horizon
- Infall proceeds smoothly
- No invariant divergence at r = 2M

**What this means:**

Schwarzschild coordinate singularity at r = 2M is **coordinate artifact**, not physical.

**Observer choice:** Schwarzschild coordinates (singular at horizon) vs E-F coordinates (regular).

**Implication:** What appears singular to one observer is regular to another.

---

### Toy 8: Painlevé-Gullstrand Slicing

**Test:** Schwarzschild spacetime in P-G coordinates (flowing space picture).

**Result:**
- Continuous infall velocity through horizon
- No freezing of infalling observers
- Spatial slices encode flow interpretation

**What this means:**

You can describe black hole spacetime as "flowing space" or "curved static geometry."

**Observer choice determines interpretation.**

**Implication:** Physical description is observer-dependent.

---

### Toy 9: Radial Infall Proper Time

**Test:** Infalling observer crosses black hole horizon.

**Result:**
- Finite proper time to horizon crossing (τ_horizon finite)
- Divergent Schwarzschild coordinate time (t → ∞)
- No physical slowdown detected by infalling observer

**What this means:**

"Time to cross horizon" depends on whose time:
- Schwarzschild observer: Infinite
- Infalling observer: Finite

**Implication:** Time itself is observer-dependent.

## Phase III: Rotation and Energy

### Toy 12: Kerr Ergosphere

**Test:** Rotating black hole, identify static limit.

**Result:**
- Static observers impossible inside ergosphere
- Timelike worldlines forced to co-rotate
- Static limit ≠ horizon

**What this means:**

Whether you can "remain at rest" depends on observer choice.

Inside ergosphere: No timelike Killing vector → cannot define "static" observer.

**Implication:** "At rest" is observer-dependent near rotating black holes.

---

### Toy 13: Kerr ISCO Efficiency

**Test:** Energy extraction efficiency from accretion onto rotating black hole.

**Result:**
- Accretion efficiency increases with spin (up to ~42% for extremal Kerr)
- Energy extraction observer- and orbit-dependent

**What this means:**

"How much energy can be extracted" depends on:
1. Which orbit (ISCO location)
2. Which observer measures energy

**Implication:** Energy extraction is observer-dependent.

---

### Toy 15: Superradiance

**Test:** Wave scattering off rotating black hole in ergoregion.

**Result:**
- Negative energy states inside ergoregion
- Outgoing modes amplified

**What this means:**

"Energy" of wave modes depends on observer frame.

Inside ergoregion: Negative energy states exist (from perspective of asymptotic observer).

**Implication:** Energy is not intrinsic to field configuration but depends on observer frame.

## Phase VIII: Horizon Thermodynamics

### Toy 54: Hawking Temperature

**Test:** Quantum fields near Schwarzschild black hole horizon.

**Result:**
- Temperature T_H = ℏc³/(8πGMk_B)
- Thermal radiation detected by asymptotic observer
- No temperature intrinsic to geometry (Ricci scalar = 0)

**What this means:**

Black hole has temperature, but:
1. Not encoded in classical geometry (R = 0 everywhere)
2. Depends on quantum state (vacuum choice)
3. Observer-dependent (asymptotic observer sees thermal; infalling sees vacuum)

**Implication:** Temperature is observer-dependent emergent phenomenon.

## Phase IX: Unruh Effect

### Toy 66: Unruh Temperature

**Test:** Uniformly accelerating observer in Minkowski vacuum.

**Setup:**
- Spacetime: Flat Minkowski (no curvature)
- Quantum state: Minkowski vacuum |0_M⟩
- Observer: Uniformly accelerated with proper acceleration a

**Result:**

```
Inertial observer:
- Sees vacuum state |0_M⟩
- Zero temperature T = 0
- No particles

Accelerated observer (acceleration a):
- Sees thermal bath
- Temperature T_U = ℏa/(2πck_B)
- Detects particles
```

**What this means:**

Temperature and particle content are **observer-dependent**, not intrinsic to quantum state.

**Same quantum state, different observers, different physics.**

**Analogy:**

This is NOT like Doppler shift (different frequencies but same photon).

This is: Different observers **disagree on number of particles**.

**Implication:**

1. Temperature is not a property of the state alone
2. Particle content is not observer-independent
3. "Vacuum" is observer-dependent concept

**This is decisive evidence that thermality is emergent and observer-conditional.**

## Phase XI: Cosmological Horizon

### Toy 71: de Sitter Horizon Complementarity

**Test:** Cosmological horizon in de Sitter spacetime (positive Λ).

**Setup:**
- Spacetime: de Sitter with cosmological constant Λ
- Horizon radius: r_H ~ (3/Λ)^(1/2)
- Question: Do different observers agree on horizon thermality?

**Result:**

```
Static observer (at origin):
- Detects thermal radiation
- Temperature T_dS = ℏ√(Λ/3)/(2πk_B)
- Sees thermal Gibbons-Hawking radiation

Bunch-Davies observer (geodesic):
- Does NOT detect thermal radiation
- Sees vacuum state
- No temperature
```

**What this means:**

Even with same geometry (same Λ, same horizon radius):
- Static observer: Thermal
- Geodesic observer: Vacuum

**Thermality is observer-conditional.**

**Connection to Toy 66:**

Both demonstrate: **Horizons → thermality, but only for specific observers.**

| Spacetime | Horizon Type | Observer 1 | Observer 2 |
|-----------|--------------|------------|------------|
| Minkowski (Toy 66) | Rindler (acceleration) | Inertial: T=0 | Accelerated: T>0 |
| Schwarzschild (Toy 54) | Event horizon | Infalling: vacuum | Asymptotic: thermal |
| de Sitter (Toy 71) | Cosmological horizon | Geodesic: vacuum | Static: thermal |

**Pattern:** Horizon thermality is **universally observer-dependent**.

**Implication:**

Thermality is not intrinsic geometric feature. It depends on:
1. Presence of horizon (for some observer)
2. Observer's worldline relative to horizon

**This strongly suggests:** Temperature, particles, and thermality are **emergent observer-dependent descriptions**, not fundamental properties.

## What This Means

### Observer-Dependence is Physical, Not Epistemic

**Epistemic observer-dependence:**
- Different observers have different knowledge
- But reality is unique underneath
- Example: Doppler shift (frequency changes, but photon is same)

**Physical observer-dependence:**
- Different observers experience different physics
- No unique "reality" underneath
- Example: Unruh effect (different particle counts, not just different measurements)

**GR exhibits physical observer-dependence:**
- Toy 66: Different particle numbers (not just different energies)
- Toy 71: Different thermality (not just different temperatures)
- Toy 54: Different vacuum definitions (not just different phases)

**This is not measurement uncertainty or epistemic limitation.**

**This is fundamentally different physics for different observers.**

---

### Connection to Quantum Mechanics

**Standard QM observer-dependence:**
- Measurement outcome depends on basis choice
- But: All observers agree on probabilities |⟨ψ|ϕ⟩|²
- Unitarily equivalent descriptions

**GR observer-dependence:**
- Particle content depends on observer (Toy 66)
- Temperature depends on observer (Toy 54, 71)
- Not unitarily equivalent (Bogoliubov transformation)

**This goes beyond standard QM observer-dependence.**

**Implication:** Observers must be incorporated into quantum state, not external to it. (This becomes explicit in Phase XII, Toys 74-75.)

**Pressure in thermodynamics:**
- Depends on boundary conditions
- Different boundaries → different pressures
- Emergent

**Energy in GR:**

- Emergent

**Pattern matches exactly.**

## Connection to Phase XII and XIII

### Phase XII: Quantum Observers

**Phase IX-XI showed:** Classical observers see different physics (temperature, particles)

**Phase XII (Toys 74-75, 80) shows:** When observers are quantum (superposed), classical observer descriptions break down entirely:
- No unique metric for superposed observer
- No unique proper time for superposed clock
- Observer factorization becomes fundamental

**This deepens the observer-dependence:** Not just different measurements, but **no unique classical description exists**.

**This completes the pattern:** Observer perspective is fundamental to physics, not a gauge choice.
