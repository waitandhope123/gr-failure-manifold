- The right focus: Quantum fields on spacetime

  - QFT on spacetime predicts: ρ_vac ~ 10^76 GeV^4

- QFT assumes fixed spacetime background
- If spacetime emergent but QFT fundamental, still have 10^123 problem
- Cannot resolve vacuum energy while keeping "fields on spacetime"

**Verdict:** Phase XII (especially Toy 72) contradicts this version

- This is not observer-dependent or contextual
- This is a sharp numerical contradiction between two established theories
- **Both QFT and GR predict definite values that contradict each other and observation**

**What this suggests:**
Energy in GR (and QFT) is not "hard to define"—it might **not exist** as a fundamental quantity. Like temperature, it could be a **statistical property** that only makes sense when coarse-graining.

### 2. Geometry Underdetermines Physics (The Smoking Gun)

**Toy 61 was decisive, Toy 67 makes it quantitative, Toy 70 shows it's structural:**

> **Toy 61:** "Identical geometry, different stress tensors. Physics depends on quantum state."

> **Toy 67:** "Same Schwarzschild geometry supports distinct semiclassical stress tensors across {Boulware, Hartle-Hawking, Unruh}. Variance strongly increases near horizon."

> **Toy 70:** "Flat background. Local patches with near-perfect agreement still fail to determine global state. Heisenberg violations persist until full-size patch."

**Why this matters:**

In a fundamental theory, specifying the fundamental variables should uniquely determine the physics.

**Examples:**
- QM: Specify |ψ⟩ → all observables determined
- Classical EM: Specify F_μν → all physics determined
- Thermodynamics: Specify (T, P, N) → equilibrium state determined

**GR:** Specify g_μν → physics **NOT** uniquely determined

**What Toy 67 adds:**
Not just "different quantum states possible"—but **measurable variance** in stress-energy components:
- Static energy density variance ~ 7.6×10⁻⁵ at r=2.1 (near horizon)
- Variance ~ 1×10⁻⁶ scale farther out
- Same geometry, different expectations **and** different uncertainties

**What Toy 70 adds:**
Even with complete local information, you cannot reconstruct global state:
- Local patch L=2: 19.5% reconstruction error, physicality violated
- Local patch L=4: Still violates Heisenberg uncertainty
- Only full L=8 patch recovers true covariance
- **Geometry + local measurements ≠ complete specification**

**Why this points to emergence:**

If geometry were fundamental, it should encode everything. But it doesn't—quantum state matters, and even local quantum data on that geometry doesn't suffice.

This is exactly what happens when you have an **emergent description**:
- Temperature doesn't determine molecular velocities
- Average position doesn't determine individual particle states  
- Geometry doesn't determine quantum state
- **Local geometry doesn't determine global information structure**

**Verdict:** Geometry is coarse-grained information, not fundamental reality. Moreover, it's **non-local** coarse-grained information—you can't build it up from local patches.

### 3. Thermality Without Geometry (The New Smoking Gun)

**Toy 66 is decisive for the temperature/observer connection:**

**Why this is the smoking gun:**

If temperature required gravity/curvature:
- There should be no thermality in flat space
- Acceleration should just change momentum distribution
- But no—acceleration creates **genuine thermal state**

**What this means:**

Temperature is **information-theoretic/observer-dependent**, not geometric:
- Different observers (inertial vs accelerated) in **same geometry**
- Experience different thermodynamic states
- Just like Toy 5 (horizons) and Toy 47 (horizon location)
- But now with quantitative thermality measurements

**Connection to black holes:**

Hawking temperature T_H = ℏc³/(8πGMk_B) might be:
- Not "black holes emit radiation" (ontological)
- But "accelerated observers near horizons detect thermal states" (epistemological)
- Same mechanism as Unruh effect
- Geometry doesn't produce temperature—**observer state does**

**Toy 71 extends this:**
Cosmological horizons (de Sitter) also show thermality
- Not specific to black holes
- Universal property of horizons
- Always observer-dependent

**This completes the thermodynamics parallel:**
```
Thermodynamics: Temperature = coarse-grained molecular kinetic energy
GR: Temperature = coarse-grained quantum information access
Both: Entropy = counting microstates (molecules vs quantum DOFs)
```

**Implication:** If temperature is information-theoretic in flat space, and entropy is geometric (Toy 30), then **geometry itself must be information-theoretic**.

## Appendix A: Detailed Phase XI Analysis

### Toy 65: Energy Non-Uniqueness — Deep Dive

**Setup:** Schwarzschild vacuum, M = 1.0

**Two definitions:**
1. **Misner-Sharp mass:** M_MS = (r/2G)(1 - g^{ab}∇_a r ∇_b r)
   - Always gives M_MS = 1.0
   - Measures "mass interior to radius r"

2. **Brown-York energy:** E_BY = (1/8πG) ∮ (K - K_0) dA
   - Varies with radius
   - Measures "energy relative to flat reference"

**Results:**
| Radius | M_MS | E_BY | Difference | Relative Error |
|--------|------|------|------------|----------------|
| r = 6M | 1.00 | 1.10 | 0.10 | 10% |
| r = 4M | 1.00 | 1.17 | 0.17 | 17% |
| r = 3M | 1.00 | 1.27 | 0.27 | 27% |
| r = 2.5M | 1.00 | 1.38 | 0.38 | 38% |
| r = 2.1M | 1.00 | 1.64 | 0.64 | 64% |

**Key insight:** Disagreement **grows** approaching horizon, where you'd most want a good energy definition.

**Why this matters:**

Both definitions satisfy reasonable criteria:
- Reduce to Newtonian mass at infinity
- Respect symmetries
- Derive from covariant principles
- Used in published literature

**No experiment can decide between them** because energy is not gauge-invariant in GR.

**Emergence interpretation:**

Energy is like temperature:
- Multiple definitions (Kelvin, Celsius, Fahrenheit)
- All equally valid for their purposes
- Underlying reality (molecular motion) doesn't have "a temperature"
- Temperature = coarse-grained description

Similarly:
- Multiple energy definitions
- All valid for their purposes
