## Mathematical Frameworks for Emergence

### 1. Renormalization Group (RG)

**Idea:** Physics looks different at different scales

**Mechanism:**
```
Start: Microscopic theory with many DOFs
    ‚Üì (integrate out fast/small DOFs)
End: Effective theory with fewer DOFs
```

**Key concepts:**
- **Fixed points:** Scale-invariant theories
- **Relevant/irrelevant operators:** What matters at large scales
- **Universality:** Different microscopic theories ‚Üí same IR behavior

**Application to GR:**
- Einstein-Hilbert action might be IR fixed point
- Higher derivative terms irrelevant in IR
- Many different fundamental theories could give same GR at large scales

**Status:** Asymptotic safety program explores this

### 2. Holographic RG

**Idea:** Radial direction in AdS = RG scale

**Mechanism:**
```
UV (boundary): CFT with all DOFs
    ‚Üì (flow into bulk)
```

**Key insight:** Geometry itself emerges from RG flow

**Application to GR:**

- UV completion = boundary theory

**Status:** Works in AdS/CFT, unclear for realistic spacetimes

**Key feature:** Network structure creates emergent metric

**Application to GR:**

- Coarse-graining = moving to larger scales
- Continuous geometry = limit of fine network

**Status:** Toy models work, full theory unknown

**Mechanism:**

**Application to GR:**
- Each tensor = fundamental DOF
- Network = discretized spacetime
- Take continuum limit ‚Üí smooth geometry

**Status:** Active research, many concrete models
